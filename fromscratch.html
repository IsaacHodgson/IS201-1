Why Weightlifting?

Lifting weights isn't just about getting stronger. It's a great way to get your mental state right, hang with friends and getting yourself right!!

Top Training Programs

Here are some of the most effective training splits, ranked from beginner to advanced:

  1. Full Body (Beginner)
    • 3 days per week
    • Focus on compound movements
    • Great for building a solid base
  2. Upper/Lower Split (Intermediate)
    • 4 days per week
    • Alternates upper and lower body days
    • Balances volume and recovery well
  3. Push/Pull/Legs (Advanced)
    • 6 days per week
    • High volume per muscle group
    • Best for hypertrophy and serious strength gains
  4. Powerlifting Program (Elite)
    • Focused on squat, bench, and deadlift
    • Periodized training cycles
    • Maximizes raw strength output

The Big Lifts

These are the foundational compound movements every serious lifter should master:

🏋️ Squat

The king of all lifts. Builds legs, glutes, and core stability.

💪 Bench Press

The standard for upper body strength. Chest, shoulders, triceps.

🔩 Deadlift

Total body strength. Back, hamstrings, and grip all in one.

⬆️ Overhead Press

Shoulder and tricep dominant. Builds impressive upper body mass.
